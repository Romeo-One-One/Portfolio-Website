My Story

Why do I practice photography?

Hi there. If you'd like to know more about who I am and how I got started with photography, please read on.


My story began...

When I was seventeen, I kicked off my senior year of high school on a pretty strong foot, but out of nowhere one of my aunts fell ill. Death, having no favorites, pried her away from us.

Now, every time my family comes together, her memory still lingers. Time has somewhat numbed my pain, but there's still a frustrating emptiness that comes from her absence, and a powerlessness to change the fact.

I've coped by trying to focus on other things, but I can only imagine how my other aunts and uncles must feel. They've known her for all their lives.

Requiescat in Pace, Auntie.

I had to face a hard reality about a girl I used to hold dearly in memory. I didn't want to accept it, but she and I weren't the same starry-eyed young middle schoolers that we used to be.

I'd been chasing a long-gone memory.

I was frustrated with myself. I'd wasted a grand deal of time, which in retrospect could have been spent nurturing other friendships, developing more skills, and studying more intensely. But hey, c'est la vie, non?

I needed something new to focus on.

My cousins took a couple of photos during the funeral, and just out of curiosity, I tried my hand at editing them to see if I could look make them look old. They weren't spectacular, but I enjoyed the process so much that I thought to myself, why not try practicing this a little more seriously?

The end of a chapter.

I can't help but laugh at how punchy the colors are, but to my old untrained eyes this shot looked pretty cool. It's so satisfying to see the evolution from my old photographs to my more recent ones.

Starting out, I used to fawn over the dreamy bokeh and image quality that you get with more expensive cameras, but I know that I can still get great results with my camera as long as I light my subjects well. Regardless of the camera I've used, practicing photography has been a wonderful way to meet people.

The start of a new chapter.

My name is Remy Dinh, but everybody calls me...

Remy.
