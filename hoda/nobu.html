Humans of De Anza: Nobu

by Remus Adrien

Posted on October 23, 2015 at 11:45 AM

Having grown up in Japan, what do you miss most about life in Japan compared to here in the United States?

"Hmm. I’m going think about it for a second.

Okay, one of the main things that I miss most about Japan is… it’s hard to describe.

Well, I’ll try to describe it:

People are very honest and very nice in Japan; you’ll see kids are running around, and I used to be one of them, just running around in the streets. There would be grandparents walking with a stroller or a cart, and you see them buying groceries. I think it’s those everyday lifestyles or occurrences; those very simple things that we see in our everyday life in Japan. People dressing up to go to the city, or just the really good food in Japan. Just seeing the people in Japan living in a society which has a really rich culture and history. That’s one of the things I miss the most.

Yeah, Japan has a very unique culture, and I’m very proud to be Japanese in a lot of ways. People are very respectful and very polite, but at the same time are very proud of who they are and very proud of the culture of Japan. They do have very rich culture and food, and arts, craftswork. People are very dedicated to what they do. A lot of people, traditionally speaking, find one occupation and they stick to that occupation until they retire. Some people are very dedicated, such as candymakers, or they can be experienced mask makers who’ve been in the field for three generations. They learn how to create and design beautiful masks from their father, who previously learned from their fathers. So this knowledge and skills, the so-called culture of the family that’s been passed on from generation to generation, that’s one of the things that I really respect about Japan and really miss, because we really don’t see that here in the United States."

- Nobu T.
